Vegetarian Lunch of the day

Garden Gala Grains

Vibrant. Verdant. Vegan. Victory.

Introducing "Garden Gala Grains," a symphony of fresh, wholesome ingredients that come together to create a hipster gourmet experience for the conscientious food lover. This vibrant vegetarian lunch is not only a visual feast for the eyes but a gastronomical delight that will satisfy your taste buds and your eco-friendly spirit.

Working Time: 20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 150g mixed salad greens
  • 200g quinoa
  • 150g cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 large cucumber, diced
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • A handful of fresh basil leaves, torn
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 30ml extra virgin olive oil
  • A pinch of sea salt
  • A sprinkle of ground black pepper

Cooking Time: 15 minutes

Difficulty: Easy

Number of Servings: 4

Kcal per Serving: Approximately 350 kcal

Preparation Steps:

  1. Begin by rinsing 200g of quinoa under cold water. Bring a pot of water with double the amount of quinoa to a boil, add the quinoa, lower the heat, and simmer for 15 minutes or until quinoa is fluffy and transparent. Drain any excess water and set aside to cool slightly.
  2. While the quinoa is cooking, prepare the vegetables. Wash the mixed greens and pat dry. Halve the cherry tomatoes, dice the cucumber, and slice the avocado.
  3. In a large salad bowl, combine the mixed salad greens, cooled quinoa, cherry tomatoes, and cucumber.
  4. Drizzle the juice of 1 lemon and 30ml of extra virgin olive oil over the salad. Season with sea salt and ground black pepper to taste.
  5. Gently toss the salad to ensure the dressing coats every ingredient evenly.
  6. Just before serving, add sliced avocado and torn fresh basil leaves to retain their vibrant color and fresh texture.
  7. Serve immediately into individual bowls or plates to preserve the aesthetic appeal of the dish.

Tip:

To elevate "Garden Gala Grains," consider adding a sprinkle of roasted seeds such as pumpkin or sunflower for an added crunch. For those who enjoy a hint of sweetness, a drizzle of balsamic glaze can work wonders. To keep it seasonal, feel free to swap out ingredients for local, in-season vegetables to ensure maximum freshness and support sustainable eating practices.

Vegan Breakfast of the day

Morning Meadow Mingle

Start your day with vibrant vitality

Introduction

Awaken your senses with a burst of color and flavor! The Morning Meadow Mingle is a vegan breakfast bowl that combines the rich greens of a meadow with the vibrancy of a morning sunrise. Packed with fresh vegetables, aromatic spices, and a creamy avocado touch, this gourmet recipe is designed to energize and satiate. Perfect for the conscious foodie looking to indulge in a hipster gourmet experience!

Working Time

15 minutes

Ingredients

  • Fresh spinach, 100 g
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • Cherry tomatoes, 150 g, halved
  • Sweet bell peppers, 2, diced
  • Fresh cilantro, 20 g, chopped
  • Green onions, 2, finely sliced
  • Garlic, 2 cloves, minced
  • Turmeric, 2 tsp
  • Black pepper, 1 tsp, freshly ground
  • Sea salt, 1 tsp, to taste

Cooking Time

10 minutes

Difficulty

Easy

Servings

2 servings

kcal per Serving

Approximately 300 kcal per serving (varies depending on the size of the vegetables and avocado)

Preparation Steps

  1. Begin by heating a large non-stick pan over medium heat while you prepare the vegetables.
  2. Wash and drain the spinach, and set aside.
  3. Cut the avocado in half, remove the pit, slice it, and scoop out the flesh with a spoon.
  4. Halve the cherry tomatoes and dice the sweet bell peppers. Finely slice the green onions and chop the cilantro.
  5. Add a dash of water to the heated pan (or use a teaspoon of olive oil if preferred), along with the minced garlic and turmeric. Sauté for about 1 minute until the garlic is fragrant, stirring constantly to avoid burning.
  6. Mix in the spinach, cherry tomatoes, and sweet bell peppers. Sauté for about 3 to 5 minutes, or until the spinach has wilted and the tomatoes are slightly soft but still vibrant.
  7. Season with sea salt and freshly ground black pepper. Toss the vegetables to ensure even seasoning.
  8. Divide the sautéed vegetables evenly between two bowls. Arrange the avocado slices on top and garnish with the green onions and chopped cilantro.

Tip

To add more texture and nutrients, sprinkle some toasted seeds (pumpkin or sunflower) or nuts (walnuts or almonds) on top of the bowl before serving. For a hint of spice, drizzle with a little hot sauce or a dollop of vegan yogurt for creaminess. You can also easily turn this into a warm salad by serving over quinoa or brown rice for a more filling meal.

Smoothie of the day

Tropical Treasure Twist Smoothie

Invigorate Your Island Spirit

A sip of this Tropical Treasure Twist Smoothie is like basking in a lush paradise, where every gulp offers a burst of vibrant, refreshing flavors. Meticulously blended to combine the rich tapestry of tropical fruits with a hint of spice and a power-packed green, this smoothie is not just a beverage—it's an experience. It caters to your health, tantalizes your taste buds, and is the perfect elixir for those seeking a gourmet, hipster twist on their daily fruit intake.

Working Time: 10 minutes

Ingredients:

  • Pineapple, fresh, 150 grams
  • Mango, ripe, 1 medium (approx. 150 grams)
  • Coconut milk, 200 ml
  • Banana, 1 large
  • Spinach, fresh, 30 grams
  • Ginger, fresh, 5 grams (peeled and grated)
  • Mint leaves, fresh, 10 leaves
  • Lime, juice of 1 medium
  • Chia seeds, 10 grams
  • Vanilla extract, 1 teaspoon

Blending Time: 2 minutes

Resting Time: Optional, 5 minutes to allow flavors to meld

Difficulty: Easy

Number of Servings: 2 servings

Kcal per Serving: Approximately 250 kcal

Preparation Steps:

  1. Prepare your fruits: Peel and roughly chop the pineapple, mango, banana, and grate the ginger.
  2. Into a blender, add the pineapple, mango, banana, and ginger.
  3. Wash the spinach and mint leaves, shake off any excess water, and add them to the blender.
  4. Pour in the coconut milk and add the chia seeds along with the vanilla extract.
  5. Squeeze in the juice of one lime, catching any seeds.
  6. Blend on high until all components are completely smooth, which typically takes about 1-2 minutes.
  7. Taste the smoothie and adjust the tartness or sweetness if necessary by adding a touch more lime juice or a drizzle of honey or agave syrup if needed.
  8. Let the smoothie sit for 5 minutes if you have time, which allows the chia seeds to swell and the flavors to develop.
  9. Give it a quick stir and then pour the smoothie into two glasses.
  10. Garnish with a sprig of mint or a few small chunks of pineapple or mango, if desired.

Tip:

For a frosty touch, you can freeze the pineapple and mango chunks prior to blending, or add a cup of ice into the mix. To enhance the nutritional profile, toss in a scoop of your favorite protein powder or a teaspoon of spirulina for an extra health kick. If you prefer your smoothie to be more liquid, simply add more coconut milk or a splash of coconut water to reach your desired consistency.

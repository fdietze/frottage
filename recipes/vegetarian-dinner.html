Vegetarian Dinner of the day

Harmony Herb Harvest

Wholesome Warmth, Hearty Health

Harmony Herb Harvest is more than just a meal; it's an aromatic adventure that delights the senses and nourishes the soul. Tapping into the roots of homely cooking, this dish weaves together a tapestry of earthy spices, vibrant vegetables, and lush legumes. It's perfect for those seeking a touch of gourmet flair in their home cooking while remaining deeply satisfying and comforting.

Working Time: 30 minutes
Cooking Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 55 minutes
Difficulty: Medium
Servings: 4
Kcal Per Serving: ~450 kcal

Ingredients:

  • Sweet potatoes, 600 grams, peeled and cubed
  • Chickpeas, 400 grams (cooked or canned)
  • Spinach, 200 grams, washed and chopped
  • Garlic, 2 cloves, minced
  • Ginger, a 2 cm piece, grated
  • Turmeric, 1 teaspoon
  • Cumin seeds, 1 teaspoon
  • Fresh coriander leaves, a small bunch, chopped
  • Red bell pepper, 1 large, diced
  • Coconut milk, 400 ml

Preparation Steps:

  1. Heat a large pot over medium heat. Dry-roast the cumin seeds until they start to pop and release their aroma, about 1-2 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add a splash of water (or light oil for a richer version), followed by the minced garlic and grated ginger. SautÃ© for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the turmeric and cumin seeds for 30 seconds to reveal their flavors.
  4. Add the sweet potatoes and red bell pepper, sautÃ©ing for another 5 minutes until they start to soften.
  5. Pour in the coconut milk and bring the mixture to a low boil. Reduce the heat to simmer and cover. Cook until the sweet potatoes are almost tender, about 15 minutes.
  6. Add the chickpeas and continue to simmer for another 5 minutes.
  7. Fold in the spinach and cook until it wilts in the warm stew, for about 2 minutes.
  8. Serve the Harmony Herb Harvest hot, garnished with fresh coriander leaves.

Tips to Improve:

  • For an extra flavor dimension, add a touch of smoked paprika along with the turmeric.
  • To make this dish even more substantial, serve it over a bed of quinoa or brown rice.
  • For a nuttier twist, sprinkle toasted almond flakes before serving.
  • To enhance the harmony of flavors, let the prepared dish rest for 10 minutes before serving, allowing the spices to further meld with the other ingredients.

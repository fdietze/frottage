Vegetarian Dinner of the day

Verdant Valley Vittles

Embrace Effortless Earthy Eating

Short Introduction

Embark on a culinary journey with this vibrant and nutritious vegetarian ensemble that's as pleasing to the eye as it is to the palate. Each ingredient in the Verdant Valley Vittles has been specifically chosen to create a symphony of flavors that are sure to bring delight and satisfaction to health-conscious gourmets and hipster foodies alike. This dish incorporates a refreshing blend of greens, hearty mushrooms, and a kick of bold spices, all unified under the banner of wholesome goodness.

Working Time:

15 minutes

Ingredients

  • Fresh spinach - 100g
  • Red bell peppers - 2, medium-sized, thinly sliced
  • Cherry tomatoes - 150g, halved
  • Cremini mushrooms - 200g, sliced
  • Avocado - 1, sliced
  • Fresh cilantro - a handful, chopped
  • Garlic - 2 cloves, minced
  • Extra virgin olive oil - 3 tablespoons
  • Ground cumin - 1 teaspoon
  • Chili flakes - 1/2 teaspoon
  • Sea salt - to taste

Cooking/Baking Time:

10 minutes

Resting Time:

None

Difficulty:

Easy

Number of Servings:

2

Kcal per Serving:

Approximately 400 kcal

Preparation Steps:

  1. Heat a large non-stick skillet over medium heat and add 2 tablespoons of olive oil.

  2. Add the minced garlic to the skillet and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.

  3. Introduce the sliced cremini mushrooms to the pan, stirring frequently, until they begin to soften and brown, around 5 minutes.

  4. Toss in the sliced bell peppers, continue to cook for an additional 3 minutes until they are tender but still crisp.

  5. Sprinkle the ground cumin and chili flakes over the veggies, stir well to evenly coat, and sauté for another 2 minutes. Season with sea salt to taste.

  6. Incorporate the fresh spinach and halved cherry tomatoes into the skillet, cooking until the spinach wilts and the tomatoes are warm but retain their shape, which should take no more than 2 minutes.

  7. Take the skillet off the heat. Gently fold in the sliced avocado and chopped cilantro, allowing the residual heat to warm them through.

  8. To serve, divide the mixture onto plates, ensuring an equal share of the vibrant, colorful veggies and avocado in each portion.

Tip to vary or improve the recipe:

For an additional protein boost and a nutty crunch, sprinkle a handful of toasted pine nuts or pumpkin seeds over each serving. If you fancy a hit of refreshing zestiness, add a squeeze of fresh lime juice just before serving. To make this dish more substantial, serve it on a bed of quinoa or alongside a slice of freshly baked artisanal bread. Enjoy your Verdant Valley Vittles as a celebration of simplicity and health.

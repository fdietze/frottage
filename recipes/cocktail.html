Cocktail of the day

Midnight Mischief Mix

An enchanting noir indulgence

Sip into the velvety embrace of the night with "Midnight Mischief Mix" – an enigmatic blend meant for whispering secrets under a star-studded sky. With its deep, luxurious hue and a caress of spice and sweetness, this cocktail is perfect for those who revel in the romance of the twilight hours.

  • Preparation Time: 10 minutes
  • Cooking Time: None
  • Resting Time: Optional, 2-3 minutes for flavors to meld
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Servings: 2
  • Calories per serving: Approximately 200 kcal

Ingredients:

  • Blackberries, 100g
  • Fresh mint leaves, a small bunch
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 vanilla pod
  • Dark rum, 100ml
  • Fresh lime juice, 30ml (from about 1 medium lime)
  • Ginger root, 2cm piece, peeled
  • Raw sugar, 50g (for simple syrup)
  • Sparkling water, 200ml

Preparation Steps:

  1. To make the simple syrup, combine the sugar with 100ml of water in a saucepan. Add the ginger root, cinnamon stick, and star anise. Gently heat until the sugar is completely dissolved. Allow to cool, then strain the syrup to remove the spices.
  2. Muddle the blackberries with the vanilla pod (seeds scraped out and pod included) and mint leaves in the base of a cocktail shaker until you've extracted the juices and the scent of vanilla and mint is released.
  3. Add the dark rum, lime juice, and 30ml of the spiced simple syrup. Fill the shaker with ice and shake vigorously.
  4. Strain into two glasses filled with crushed ice, topping each with sparkling water.
  5. Garnish with a few whole blackberries, a sprig of mint, and a thin slice of lime.

Difficulty Level: Easy

This recipe is perfect for amateur mixologists looking to impress with a sophisticated and stylish drink without needing too much expertise.

Number of Servings: 2 servings

The given measurements will conjure up two generous potions of Midnight Mischief Mix, ideal for sharing with a companion.

Kcal per Serving: Approximately 200 kcal

Each potion contains about 200 kcal, depending on the exact size and ripeness of the ingredients used.

Tip to Vary or Improve the Recipe:

Play with different berries or try adding a splash of Chambord for a raspberry hint. For a non-alcoholic version, omit the rum and add a touch more lime juice and ginger – the spiced simple syrup will still make it a treat! Enhance the presentation by serving in chilled, frosted glasses for an added touch of refined mischief.
